## Decision

In the current phase, chain-only quarantines do not enter canonical lifecycle migration.

## Why

- Repo law currently freezes `strategy_key` as the sole governance key.
- Chain-only quarantines do not currently have a trustworthy, auditable strategy-attribution source.
- Hard-mapping them to an existing `strategy_key` would fabricate governance truth.
- Introducing a new attribution surface inside this packet would widen governance/schema law without explicit approval.
- The current phase should prefer phase honesty and low blast radius.

## Immediate effect

- Current and immediately adjacent canonical lifecycle migration packets may not write chain-only quarantines into canonical lifecycle truth.
- No packet may invent, infer, borrow, or backfill any existing `strategy_key` for these positions.
- No packet may add a new governance attribution field, enum, schema surface, or reader contract under this resolution.
- Chain-only quarantines must be treated as:
  - excluded from canonical migration
  - pending future governance design
  - visible as a non-canonical exception

## Required follow-through in the successor packet

The successor packet must freeze and then land all of the following without widening governance law:
- exact exclusion boundary for what counts as a chain-only quarantine
- explicit visibility rule so excluded objects do not silently disappear
- downstream handling rule so audit / risk / review surfaces know the objects were excluded rather than absent
- explicit reopen condition stating that only a later approved governance-design packet may revisit canonical inclusion

## Non-goals

- finding a replacement `strategy_key` now
- expanding schema or law now
- solving the long-term governance attribution model now
- pretending canonical migration is fully closed

## Risk note

The main risk of this decision is an observability blind spot.
Therefore the exclusion must stay explicit; it may not degrade into a silent skip.

## Human gate

Only a future approved governance-design packet may change this decision.
